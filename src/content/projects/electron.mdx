## Electron
Electron's platform uses data from a variety of sources, including satellites, ground sensors, and weather models. This data is then processed and analyzed to provide insights into a variety of environmental indicators, such as carbon emissions, deforestation, and air quality. Electron's platform is used by a variety of organizations, including governments, businesses, and NGOs. They use it to track their progress on climate change goals, identify areas where they can reduce their carbon emissions, and make informed decisions about how to manage their resources.
[Preview](https://electron.meteory.eu/).

### Features
- Data visualization: Electron's platform provides users with a variety of tools for visualizing data, such as maps, charts, and graphs. This makes it easy to understand complex environmental data and identify trends.
- Reporting: Electron's platform generates automated reports that summarize key findings from the data. These reports can be used to track progress, identify areas for improvement, and communicate with stakeholders.
- Use cases: Electron has a library of use cases that demonstrate how its platform can be used to solve real-world problems. These use cases cover a wide range of industries, including energy, agriculture, and transportation.

### Stack
- [React](https://nextjs.org) - Next.js is a React framework for building full-stack web applications
- [TailwindCSS](https://tailwindcss.com) - A utility-first CSS framework packed with classes that can be composed to build any design, directly in your markup.
- [Zustand](https://zustand-demo.pmnd.rs/) - a small unopinionated state management library built by Jotai and React-spring.
- [Mapbox GL](https://www.mapbox.com/mapbox-gljs) - Mapbox GL JS is a JavaScript library for vector maps on the web.
